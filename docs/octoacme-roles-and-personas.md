# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

### Team Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Clarify requirements and acceptance criteria
- **Project Managers**: Provide estimates, report progress, and flag blockers
- **QA/Testing**: Collaborate on test case design and defect resolution
- **Solutions Architect**: Review technical designs and get guidance on complex problems
- **UX Designer**: Implement designs and provide feedback on feasibility
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in agile ceremonies and raise impediments

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

### Team Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Align on priorities, timeline, and resource constraints
- **Developers**: Define requirements, review implementations, and validate outcomes
- **UX Designer**: Collaborate on user research and design direction
- **Data Analyst**: Review metrics and analytics to inform product decisions
- **Customer Success Specialist**: Gather customer feedback and prioritize requests
- **Solutions Architect**: Validate technical feasibility of product requirements

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

### Team Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Coordinate on roadmap, priorities, and scope decisions
- **Developers**: Track progress, manage blockers, and facilitate planning
- **Scrum Master**: Align on team process, velocity, and sprint health
- **Solutions Architect**: Identify and mitigate technical risks and dependencies
- **Data Analyst**: Track project metrics and success criteria
- **Customer Success Specialist**: Coordinate customer-impacting releases

---

## QA/Testing

### Role Summary
QA/Testing professionals validate quality and ensure that deliverables meet acceptance criteria. They work closely with developers and product teams to identify defects, edge cases, and quality improvements.

### Responsibilities
- Perform manual and automated testing
- Validate acceptance criteria before release
- Identify edge cases and quality issues
- Contribute to test automation and quality processes
- Document and track bugs and defects
- Participate in acceptance testing and user acceptance testing (UAT)

### Goals
- Ensure high-quality releases with minimal defects
- Reduce time to find and fix issues
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Maintain clear quality gates and standards

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- Bug reports and test case documentation
- QA status updates in sprint reviews
- Collaboration with developers on defect resolution

### Team Interactions
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test case design, defect triage, and fixes
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and feature behavior
- **Project Managers**: Report on testing progress, risks, and quality metrics
- **UX Designer**: Verify that implementations match design specifications

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create intuitive, user-centered experiences by researching user needs, designing interfaces, and validating designs through testing. They ensure that products are usable, accessible, and delightful.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- Design user interfaces and interaction patterns
- Collaborate with developers to ensure design implementation
- Maintain design systems and component libraries
- Validate designs with users and stakeholders

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction and improve user satisfaction
- Ensure consistency across the product
- Balance user needs with business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Wireframes, mockups, and prototypes
- User research findings and recommendations
- Collaboration with developers on implementation details

### Team Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Understand user needs and prioritize design work
- **Developers**: Collaborate on feasibility and implementation
- **QA/Testing**: Ensure design is implemented correctly
- **Customer Success Specialist**: Gather feedback on user pain points and satisfaction
- **Data Analyst**: Use analytics to inform design decisions

---

## Data Analyst

### Role Summary
Data Analysts collect, analyze, and interpret data to inform decision-making and measure product performance. They provide insights that guide product strategy, feature prioritization, and optimization efforts.

### Responsibilities
- Define and track key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Analyze user behavior and product usage patterns
- Create dashboards and reports for stakeholders
- Conduct A/B tests and experimentation analysis
- Identify trends, anomalies, and opportunities
- Translate data into actionable insights

### Goals
- Enable data-driven decision making
- Measure and improve product performance
- Identify opportunities for growth and optimization
- Ensure data accuracy and accessibility

### Typical Communication
- Weekly metrics reviews and data briefings
- Dashboard updates and analytical reports
- Experiment results and recommendations
- Ad-hoc analysis for product decisions

### Team Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Provide insights to inform roadmap and prioritization
- **Developers**: Collaborate on instrumentation and data collection
- **UX Designer**: Share user behavior data to inform design decisions
- **Project Managers**: Report on project metrics and success criteria
- **Customer Success Specialist**: Analyze customer usage and satisfaction data

---

## Solutions Architect

### Role Summary
Solutions Architects design technical solutions that meet business requirements while ensuring scalability, security, and maintainability. They bridge the gap between business needs and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Design system architecture and integration patterns
- Evaluate technology options and make recommendations
- Ensure solutions meet non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability)
- Create technical specifications and architecture diagrams
- Review and approve technical designs
- Identify and mitigate technical risks

### Goals
- Deliver scalable, maintainable technical solutions
- Balance business needs with technical constraints
- Ensure architectural consistency across projects
- Reduce technical debt and complexity

### Typical Communication
- Architecture review boards and design sessions
- Technical design documents and diagrams
- Risk assessments and mitigation plans
- Consultation with development teams on complex problems

### Team Interactions
- **Developers**: Guide implementation and review technical designs
- **Product Managers**: Translate business requirements into technical solutions
- **Project Managers**: Identify technical dependencies and risks
- **Scrum Master**: Participate in planning to address technical constraints
- **Data Analyst**: Design data architecture and analytics infrastructure

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile practices and remove impediments to help teams deliver value efficiently. They coach teams on agile principles, facilitate ceremonies, and foster continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach team members on agile practices and principles
- Track team velocity and sprint metrics
- Shield the team from external distractions
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and productivity
- Maintain healthy team dynamics and collaboration
- Ensure adherence to agile principles and practices
- Continuously improve team processes

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action items and follow-ups
- Impediment escalations and resolution tracking
- Velocity and burndown reports

### Team Interactions
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on project timeline, risks, and cross-team dependencies
- **Product Managers**: Facilitate backlog refinement and sprint planning
- **Developers**: Remove blockers and enable efficient delivery
- **QA/Testing**: Ensure quality processes are integrated into sprint workflow
- **Solutions Architect**: Coordinate on technical planning and dependencies

---

## Customer Success Specialist

### Role Summary
Customer Success Specialists ensure customers achieve their desired outcomes while using the product. They gather feedback, advocate for customer needs, and help drive product improvements based on real-world usage.

### Responsibilities
- Onboard new customers and provide ongoing support
- Monitor customer health metrics and engagement
- Gather and document customer feedback and pain points
- Advocate for customer needs in product discussions
- Identify opportunities for upsells and expansions
- Track customer satisfaction and retention metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer satisfaction and retention
- Reduce churn and increase product adoption
- Provide actionable feedback to product teams
- Build strong customer relationships

### Typical Communication
- Customer check-ins and business reviews
- Support tickets and escalations
- Feedback reports and feature requests
- Success stories and case studies

### Team Interactions
- **Product Managers**: Share customer feedback and advocate for feature requests
- **UX Designer**: Provide insights on user pain points and usability issues
- **Data Analyst**: Review customer usage data and health metrics
- **Project Managers**: Coordinate on customer-impacting releases and timelines
- **Developers**: Escalate critical customer issues and bugs
